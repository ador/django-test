New Data Scientists Wanted!

Participate in the BUdapest BIke Sharing (BUBI) Analytics Challenge Organized by the "Big Data - Momentum" research group of the Hungarian Academy of Sciences (MTA SZTAKI) and the Centre for Budapest Transport (BKK).

The Municipality of Budapest made a decision in 2008 to establish the public bike-sharing scheme with an initial location of 76 docking stations. The MOL BUBI system went live in September 2014 with 1100 bicycles and 76 docking stations. You can use BUBI with annual, semi-annual passes or one, three and seven day tickets, 24 hours every days.

In the Analytics Challenge, we use 2 months of data to predict usage patterns in three tasks, two for predictive analytics and one for open research.

Tasks

Task 1: Busiest link prediction

Predict the busiest links (pairs of source, destination docking stations) for given evaluation periods (parts of certain days), excluding service travels. Predictions will be evaluated by NDCG by using the following formula

Task 2: Maintenance prediction

For each day in the evaluation set, predict the time when a docking station would become empty without service reallocation of the bikes. You may also predict "never", meaning that no service travels are required to the given station at the given day. Predictions will be evaluated by the RMSE of the logarithmic difference in seconds. Error in the predictions of "never" count as a difference of 24 hours.

Task 3: Open research task

In this task, you may participate by submitting a very brief research plan. Goals may include, but not limited to, visualization or use of additional external data. You are expected to keep in regular contact with the organizers to share and discuss your findings.

Who may participate?

We expect teams of B.Sc., M.Sc. or Ph.D. students of computer science, mathematics, physics, transportation, ..., but participation is open. Money prizes have priority to go for the student teams.
